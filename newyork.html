Empire State of Mind

An account of a summer of New York City.

I’m feeling an extremely strange mix of feelings. Maybe I’m just experiencing post New York syndrome, maybe I’m feeling wistful that another chapter has come to a close, that I’m cycling through life at such a strange speed and direction, racing through people, places, time. The city has changed me in ways I struggle to describe. If I had to summarize, I would say these three months has been a manifestation of the slogan plastered on the office wall – living the dream.

Living the Dream Part I:

The Carrot and Stick Broken

Arriving

I arrived in Manhattan, to a 35th floor high-rise corner apartment overlooking the Hudson with views of the Empire State Building, Times Square, and the Statue of Liberty in the distance – a million dollar view, an unembellished description. If there were a movie about some secret agent spy living in Manhattan, this would be her apartment.

This was only the beginning of a series of luxuries I would have never imagined I would touch. From endless raw coconut water and a wide variety of teas in the office, to free massages every week, to subsidized gym membership and $30 seamless sushi orders, there was hardly anything – within reason – I could have asked for that I couldn’t get.

Lessons

All this is great, but for those that have gotten to where they were via blood and sweat, those who have lived under more modest means – not saying I completely fall into this category – this is one itching problem with this – that your internal rewards system is broken. I’m not commenting on the bigger picture – whatever career ambitions, greater sources of meaning in life, may still very well be on a great track – but on a granular level, when you’ve come to take luxuries as an expectation, your happiness and enjoyment levels adjust, and it takes more to bring you the same level of satisfaction. Water is the most glorious after you’ve run 10 miles under the blistering heat. Fancy teas taste like shit when your fridge has hundreds of them.
