Empire State of Mind





Summer 2014 has been a period of restless exploration. I may be just experiencing post New York syndrome right now, or I may be feeling wistful that another chapter has come to a close, but I feel like I’m cycling through life at a strange speed and direction, racing through people, places, time. The city has changed me in ways I struggle to describe. If I had to summarize, I would say these three months has been a manifestation of the slogan plastered on the office wall – living the dream.

Part I:

The Carrot and Stick Broken

A million dollar view

I arrived to an apartment on the 35th floor of a high rise building in Manhattan. It overlooked the Hudson River, and had views of the Empire State Building, Times Square, and the Statue of Liberty. This was a view cityscape photographers dreamed of, a view my tiny iPhone 5 camera could do no justice for.

This was only the beginning of a series of luxuries I would have never imagined I would touch. From endless raw coconut water to lobster tails, to massages every week, gym membership, and unrestricted seamless orders, there was hardly anything within reason I could not have asked for.

But water is the most glorious after you’ve run 10 miles under the blistering heat, and organic green tea ice cream tastes terrible after you've stuffed yourself full in an air-conditioned office building. Material pleasures are nice, but they saturate, and the greatest danger I find with them is that they can break your internal rewards system. Happiness adapts to expectations and while I appreciate the opportunity to have all that I can have, the most rewarding moments in life come when you have close to nothing.

After all, the rich, while having lived richly, had actually lived poorly
And the poor, while having lived poorly, has actually lived richly.

Love people, use things. Not the other way around.
